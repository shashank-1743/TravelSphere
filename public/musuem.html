Welcome to Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial, located in Kolkata, India, is a majestic marble monument dedicated to Queen Victoria. Completed in 1921, it stands as a splendid example of British colonial architecture, blending elements of Indian, Mughal, and European styles. Surrounded by lush gardens and a serene lake, the memorial houses a museum and art gallery showcasing a rich collection of artifacts, paintings, and sculptures from the British era. As one of Kolkata's most iconic landmarks, it offers visitors a glimpse into the grandeur of colonial history and remains a popular destination for tourists and history enthusiasts alike.

Features

  • Central Dome: The central dome of the memorial is 56 meters high and is adorned with a bronze statue of Queen Victoria.
  • Gardens: The memorial is surrounded by sprawling gardens and a large artificial lake, providing a scenic and tranquil environment.
  • Museum and Art Gallery: The interior of the memorial houses a museum and art gallery with a collection of paintings, sculptures, and historical artifacts from the British era.
